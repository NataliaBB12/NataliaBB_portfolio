## Challenges

### #1 — A 15-Step Flow Designed for Ideal Conditions
<T en="The original kiosk flow assumed a calm, focused user with time to read. In practice, passengers at check-in are stressed, carrying luggage, watching the clock, and often traveling in groups — a context where every unnecessary step compounds anxiety." es="El flujo original del kiosco asumía un usuario tranquilo y concentrado con tiempo para leer. En la práctica, los pasajeros en el check-in están estresados, cargando equipaje, mirando el reloj y frecuentemente viajando en grupo — un contexto donde cada paso innecesario multiplica la ansiedad." />

### #2 — Document Scanning: The Invisible Drop-Off Point
<T en="Field research revealed that 70% of friction happened during document scanning. Error messages were generic, provided no recovery path, and left passengers unsure whether to retry, ask for help, or abandon the kiosk entirely." es="La investigación de campo reveló que el 70% de la fricción ocurría durante el escaneo de documentos. Los mensajes de error eran genéricos, no ofrecían ninguna ruta de recuperación y dejaban a los pasajeros sin saber si reintentar, pedir ayuda o abandonar el kiosco." />

### #3 — The SABRE Constraint: 5-Passenger Hard Limit
<T en="The backend provider SABRE couldn't process check-in for more than 5 passengers per transaction. The original UI allowed up to 9 — creating a silent backend failure state with no clear error recovery." es="El proveedor de backend SABRE no podía procesar el check-in de más de 5 pasajeros por transacción. La interfaz original permitía hasta 9 — creando un estado de fallo silencioso en el backend sin una recuperación de errores clara." />

<T en="**How we solved it:** We capped the visible selection UI at 5 passengers per transaction and handled the technical reset silently in the backend. Passengers completing check-in for larger groups are guided through a second iteration of the same flow — without being exposed to the underlying limitation. The experience stays clean without requiring a SABRE-side change." es="**Cómo lo resolvimos:** Limitamos la interfaz de selección visible a 5 pasajeros por transacción y gestionamos el reinicio técnico de forma silenciosa en el backend. Los pasajeros que hacen check-in para grupos más grandes son guiados a través de una segunda iteración del mismo flujo — sin exponerlos a la limitación subyacente. La experiencia permanece limpia sin necesitar cambios en SABRE." />

## Design Process

<T en="We didn't redesign the entire 15-step flow at once. We identified the highest-friction micro-moment first, validated our solution there, and then restructured the surrounding flow around it — reducing risk and shipping faster." es="No rediseñamos todo el flujo de 15 pasos de una vez. Identificamos primero el micro-momento con mayor fricción, validamos la solución y luego reestructuramos el flujo restante alrededor de ese punto — reduciendo el riesgo y acelerando la entrega." />

1. **Audit** — Mapped the end-to-end journey across Planning, Booking, Check-in, and Day of Travel to identify where kiosk friction sat within the broader passenger experience and what was in scope.

2. **Field Research** — Conducted 48+ hours of guerrilla usability testing and direct observation at the terminal. Benchmarked 18 airlines. Discovered 10 edge cases invisible in the documented flow. Identified document scanning as the primary drop-off point.

3. **Prioritize** — Rather than redesigning everything at once, prioritized the document scanning step first. Validated the improved experience, then restructured the surrounding flow around the validated anchor. This approach reduced delivery risk.

4. **Design** — Reduced steps from 15 to 6 (domestic: 2). Standardized repeated information across screens. Moved validations earlier in the flow. Added persistent progress indicators. Rewrote all instruction copy using literal, direct language.

5. **Constrain** — Collaborated with engineering to cap the passenger selection UI at 5 and handle backend resets silently. Defined a shared error taxonomy with actionable messages for every failure state.

6. **Test & Iterate** — Ran usability tests via Maze and directly at the terminal. Validated the redesigned flow against edge cases and multiple passenger scenarios to prevent future support escalations.

## Solution Implemented

<T en="A linear, predictable, and error-tolerant check-in flow — designed for passengers who are already stressed, not passengers who have time to explore." es="Un flujo de check-in lineal, predecible y tolerante a errores — diseñado para pasajeros que ya están estresados, no para quienes tienen tiempo de explorar." />

<InsightCard label="15 Steps Reduced to 6 (Domestic: 2)" labelEs="15 Pasos Reducidos a 6 (Doméstico: 2)" es="Se eliminaron los pasos redundantes, se consolidaron las pantallas con información repetida y se reestructuró el flujo para que sea lineal y secuencial. El flujo doméstico se redujo a solo 2 pasos — el camino mínimo viable hacia un pase de abordar.">
  Eliminated redundant steps, consolidated repeated information screens, and restructured the flow to be linear and sequential. The domestic flow was reduced to just 2 steps — the minimum viable path to a boarding pass.
</InsightCard>

<InsightCard label="Document Scanning Redesigned as the Anchor Step" labelEs="Escaneo de Documentos Rediseñado como Paso Ancla" es="Se reescribieron todas las instrucciones del paso de escaneo usando un lenguaje literal y directo — sin metáforas, sin íconos sin texto, sin ambigüedad. Se añadió orientación contextual que aparece exactamente cuando y donde el usuario la necesita, no antes.">
  Rewrote all instructions for the scanning step using literal, direct language — no metaphors, no icons without text, no ambiguity. Added contextual guidance that surfaces exactly when and where the user needs it, not before.
</InsightCard>

<InsightCard label="Actionable Error Messages Replacing Generic Failures" labelEs="Mensajes de Error Accionables en Lugar de Fallos Genéricos" es="Se reemplazó cada instancia de 'Ocurrió un error' con un mensaje específico que explica qué falló, por qué importa y exactamente qué debe hacer el pasajero a continuación. Se definió una taxonomía de errores compartida con ingeniería para mantener los mensajes consistentes en todos los estados de fallo.">
  Replaced every instance of "An error occurred" with a specific message explaining what failed, why it matters, and exactly what the passenger should do next. Defined a shared error taxonomy with engineering to keep messages consistent across all failure states.
</InsightCard>

<InsightCard label="5-Passenger Cap with Transparent Flow Looping" labelEs="Límite de 5 Pasajeros con Flujo Cíclico Transparente" es="Se limitó la interfaz de selección visible a 5 pasajeros para ajustarse al límite del backend SABRE. Los grupos más grandes son guiados en silencio a través de una segunda iteración del mismo flujo — experiencia limpia, sin restricción técnica expuesta.">
  Capped the visible selection UI at 5 passengers to match the SABRE backend limit. Larger groups are silently guided through a second iteration of the same flow — clean experience, no exposed technical constraint.
</InsightCard>

<InsightCard label="Progress Always Visible" labelEs="Progreso Siempre Visible" es="Se añadió un indicador de progreso persistente en todos los pasos para que los pasajeros siempre sepan dónde están, cuántos pasos quedan y que el sistema está funcionando. Reduce la ansiedad y previene el abandono prematuro.">
  Added a persistent progress indicator across all steps so passengers always know where they are, how many steps remain, and that the system is working. Reduces anxiety and prevents premature abandonment.
</InsightCard>

## Reflections

### The Context of Use Is a Design Constraint
A kiosk is not a phone. Shared hardware, time pressure, noise, luggage, and group travel change what "good UX" means completely. Designing without internalizing that context produces beautiful flows that fail in practice.

### Field Research Reveals What Documentation Cannot
48+ hours on-site uncovered 10 edge cases and identified document scanning as the real drop-off point — none of which appeared in the existing flow documentation. Time in the field is not optional for physical-context products.

### Start with the Micro-Moment, Not the Full Flow
Redesigning the document scanning step first, validating it, and then restructuring the surrounding flow reduced delivery risk and let us ship faster. Focused bets beat comprehensive overhauls.

### Technical Constraints Are a Design Brief
The SABRE 5-passenger limit was a backend constraint with no timeline for resolution. Designing around it cleanly — without exposing the limitation to users — was a design decision, not a compromise. Constraints force clarity.

### Accessibility Belongs in Round One
Accessibility considerations surfaced late in testing and required a separate design pass post-launch. Including users with visual impairments from the first research round would have prevented this. Late accessibility is a form of rework.

### Define Your Error Taxonomy on Day One
Generic error messages were the most cited pain point in user research — and the last thing we fixed. Establishing a shared error taxonomy with the engineering team from week one would have made this a first-release improvement, not a post-launch catch-up.
